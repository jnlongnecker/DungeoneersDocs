# Magic

Magic is the utilization of an energy source called mana. Mana generates from materials that match with one of the 10 elements, with quantity and purity of the material creating more dense mana. For example, a lake may produce great quantities of water mana, but if it has a large quantity of living things inside it this mana will be diluted with natural mana from the animals and wood mana from the plants and ground mana from the bed and dark mana from the depths and decay of dead things. If mana is too diluted, it scatters and never gets particularly dense. Environments in equilibrium tend to have very diluted mana.

Environments will extremely dense mana will eventually result in the manifestation of mobs which will inevitably throw the environment in further disarray as these creatures instinctually destroy mana sources that conflict with their element.

Humans are also mana sources, just like all other things. Typical humans produce natural mana, which tends to cancel itself with the vast amounts of mana produced by their societies such as fire mana, dark mana, light mana, water mana, metal mana, ground mana, and wood mana. Through training, a human is capable of increasing the density of the mana they produce as well as its element.

Magic is an art utilized by humans, mobs, and demons to control mana for themselves. The effects of casted magic (called arcana) are more limited in humans than in mobs and demons. In humans, arcana is built out of building blocks called invocations. Mana is fueled into invocations, which modify the outcome of the arcana.

Aside from moves, the most common kind of magic that humans use are enchantment and alchemy. These examples are preparatory magic that attaches a spell to an object for use later. Enchantment attaches the spell into a solid medium, while alchemy imbues it into a liquid. Both forms require the mana inherent in the components to complete the product alongside the skill and mana of the creator.

The least common kind of magic are rituals. Rituals often require components with certain kinds of mana in them and the focused practice of (often) several magi over an extended period of time to complete. Simple rituals can be done by individuals, while complex and powerful ones often require many powerful magi to have even a hope of success. The result is some of the most varied and powerful spells in known existence, but often not practical for combat or even exploration use.
