[FACT] Characters are good at some things

[FACT] Characters are bad at some things

[FACT] Player capabilities are not the same as character capabilities

[PROBLEM] How to represent character ability at a task?

[IDEA] What does being good at something _mean_?

[FACT] You are more likely to succeed at something that involves the skill

[FACT] You are more likely to produce a higher quality outcome at tasks that use the skill

[FACT] You are less likely to be incapable of accomplishing tasks that use the skill

[IDEA] More likely implies what underlying mechanic?

[FACT] Probability

[PROBLEM] How can we represent probability in a game?

[OPTION] Dice

[OPTION] Cards

[OPTION] Computer RNG

[OPTION] Blind pick

[PROBLEM] What option do we prefer?

[DECISION] Dice

[IDEA] Why?

[DECISION] Dice are readily accessible even by people that don't play TTRPs and have a small/manageable possibility space

[PROBLEM] What does being more skilled at a task mean for a dice resolution?

[OPTION] Flat bonus

- Raises minimum & maximum
- Untrained will rarely if ever beat trained
- Minimum and maximum chance never change
- Scales with guage

[OPTION] Re-roll low outcomes, take highest

- Makes minimum significantly less likely
- Makes maximum more likely
- Makes higher values way more likely
- Does not change minimum or maximum
- Untrained can beat trained, just less likely
- Threshold scales with guage

[OPTION] Re-roll low outcomes

- Makes minimum less likely
- Makes higher values more likely evenly
- Does not change minimum or maximum
- Untrained can beat trained
- Threshold scales with guage
- As threshold scales, lower values get more likely

[OPTION] Roll multiple dice

- Barely rasises minimum
- Greatly raises maximum
- Maximum & minimum are less likely
- Average is more likley
- Untrained are unlikely to beat trained
- Scales multiplicatively with guage

[OPTION] Explode dice easier

- Minimum unchanged & just as likely
- Maximum raised
- Untrained can sometimes beat trained
- Scales infinitely if falloff not enforced

[OPTION] Count success; roll under

- Maximum & minimum don't change
- Goes up evenly
- Untrained can rarely beat trained
- Threshold scales with guage
- Limited meaningful range of guage (1-[dn-1])

[QUESTION] How do threats scale over time?

[DECISION] High level characters shouldn't be seriously threatened by low level ones

[CONCLUSION] Highly skilled characters should be able to consistently roll higher than low skilled characters, and contests between the two should usually favor the more skilled character

[QUESTION] What guage scales rolls?

[OPTION] Attribute

- SPECIAL
- Strength
- Dexterity/Agility
- Constitution/Toughness
- Intelligence/Reason
- Wisdom/Intuition
- Charisma/Ego

- Inherent characteristics about the character
- Has nothing to do with "training"
  - Physically strong characters aren't necessarily good at fighting; for example
- The "mental" attributes de-sync the capabilities between player & character
- Grants role-playing insight
  - Low intelligence -> play character as an idiot

[OPTION] Stat

- Stats are trained, but seperate from other "trained" activities
- What is intrinsic, what is "trained"?

[OPTION] Skill Rank

- Unified with other "trained" activities
- Represents different levels of expertise in individual skills
- Should combat skills have the option of going untrained?

[OPTION] Binary Proficiency

- Does not reflect reality
  - Characters are not equally proficient across all trained disciplines
- Simple

[REQUIREMENT] It should not be easy to make a "wrong" build

- A "wrong" build is one that is unviably weak compared to another build
- "Easy" being a reasonable person could stumble into it in good faith
  - Could be due to not understanding the rules
  - Could be due to lack of mastery
  - Could be due to character idea that doesn't work well

[IDEA] What is the anatomy of a "wrong"/"bad" build?

- Does weak damage
- Incapable of their main role/niche
- Fighting the mechanics
- Enemies constantly resist their abilities
- Turns wasted doing non-useful things

[IDEA] How do gauges interact with "bad" builds?

[OPTION] Attribute

- Intrinsic characteristic
  - Links "I am smart" to "I must build features that express my intelligence"
  - Cannot have a character that is dumb and capable of "intelligence" linked abilities
  - Can conflict with character ideas
    - Dumb wizards
    - Weak fighters
    - Shy bards
- Requires understanding of attribute -> power capability

[OPTION] Stat

- Being proficient in "bad" stats for your character
  - Easier to do due to separation between "stat" & "stat application"

[OPTION] Skill Rank

- Being skilled in "bad" skills for your character
  - Hard, as skills are self-explanatory

[OPTION] Binary Proficiency

- Being proficient in "bad" skills
  - Hard, as skills are self-explanatory
- Does not allow for nuance & degrees of skill across several skills

[DECISION] No attributes, only skill ranks and raw stats.
