# Procedure

A `procedure` is a sequence of steps that define the process to play a section of the game. Each `procedure` has its own definition of a `round`, when it begins, and how long it lasts.

## Combat Procedure

The `combat procedure` starts when one or more `creatures` become aware of the presence of a hostile `creature` or dangerous `object` and ends when the danger clears. The map turns to the `dungeon map`, and each `round` lasts about 0.2 seconds. `Actions` may be performed.

## Exploration Procedure

The `exploration procedure` starts when traveling longer distances and ends when starting another `procedure`. The map turns to the `world map`, and each `round` lasts 1 hour. `Average` `activities` may be performed.

After each `round`, participants may choose to move to an adjacent `region`.

## Dungeon Procedure

The `dungeon procedure` starts when traveling carefully in a dangerous environment and ends when starting another `procedure`. The map turns to the `dungeon map`, and each `round` lasts about 10 minutes. `Short` `activities` may be performed.

After each `round`, participants may choose to move to an adjacent `zone`.

## Social Procedure

The `social procedure` starts when two `creatures` engage in conversation and ends when the conversation ends. The map (if one is necessary) turns to the `dungeon map`, and each `round` lasts the real time between breaks in the conversation. `Short` `activities` may be performed.

During a `social procedure`, the structure is not as rigid. If any `activities` are being performed, allow them to happen as the conversation unfolds and allow participants to react and interrupt their actions as desired. Players should be mindful of what they have asked to be doing and respect that these things will take the real time that is occuring during conversation to complete.

## Downtime Procedure

The `downtime procedure` starts when one or more `creatures` decide to rest from dungeoneering and ends when cancelled or completed (not interrupted). `Long` `activities` may be performed.

## Chase Procedure

The `chase procedure` starts when one group of `creatures` flees and another group pursues and ends when one side gives up. The map turns to the `dungeon map`, and each `round` lasts about 6 seconds. `Actions` may be performed.

After each `round`, participants may choose to move to an adjacent `zone`.

# Rounds

A `round` is when all `creatures` involved in the `procedure` utilize their `action`/`activity`. The duration of a `round` depends on the `procedure` it is in. At the start of a `round`, all `creatures` declare their `action`, and the `actions` resolve in order of `creature` `speed` (ties favor the player).
