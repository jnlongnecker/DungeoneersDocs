# Adversary Roll

`Adversary` mechanics work differently to players. When they `roll`, they instead roll 1d6 always. If they get a 1, they miss. If they get a 6, they `critical`. This is called an `adversary roll`.

Typically this is only done with `attacks`. Most of the time, if a `roll` is called for it is the `player` that makes the `roll`. When this happens, a `reaction roll` is done by the `player`.

# Type

The `type` of an `adversary` determines what other `types` it is willing to ally with, as well as common characteristics to the `type`.

# Difficulty

The `difficulty` gives insight into how tough of an opponent it is.

## Encounter Difficulty

Building an `encounter` is more of an art than a science. In order to determine how difficult an `encounter` is, you need to have a good understanding of both the capabilities of the players and their opposition.

This section will give some guidance on how to do this at a basic level, and as you gain a greater understanding of the systems in place it will become more accurate over time. To calculate the base `encounter difficulty`, apply the following algorithm:

- Add the `difficulty` of all `adversaries`
- Add the number of `adversaries`
- Subtract the number of players
- Subtract the `level` of each player

This will give you a number. Use the following table to make sense of it:

| Number      | Meaning                                                                        |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| -2 or lower | Trivially easy. It may not even be worth running                               |
| -1          | Easy. The players are very likely to win                                       |
| 0           | Average. The players will probably win, but bad circumstance can cause mishaps |
| 1           | Challenging. The players will have to utilize pretty good tactics to win       |
| 2           | Hard. The players will need excellent tactics to stand a chance                |
| 3           | Deadly. The players are likely to sustain losses or even wipe the party        |
| 4+          | Lethal. The players have almost no chance. It may not even be worth running    |
