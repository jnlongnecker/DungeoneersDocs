# Space

Space is handled in a partially abstract, partially concrete manner. The primary spacial division are ranks which are used most often when space is important to the task at hand (for example, combat procedures).

# Ranks

The battlefield is divided into 2 halves with 4 ranks each. Each rank holds (typically) a single occupant. In cramped spaces, it is limited to a maximum of 1, in narrow spaces, a maximum of 2, and in open spaces a maximum of 3.

## Moving Ranks

When you wish to move during a combat or chase procedure, you may move a number of ranks equal to your transposition (body) capability. If you are attempting to move past an obstacle, you must have a high enough remaining capability score to pass. Similarly, if you wish to move past a creature who does not want to let you past, your remaining capability score must exceed theirs.

Each rank moved consumes 1 capability score, moving past obstacles/blocking creatures consumes additional capability score equal to the number you must exceed.

# Range

When range is called into play, the range is the number of ranks the action can travel. Range can only go forwards into the opposing side and can only go backwards with special abilities.

# Zones

A zone is an area of interest and the unit traveled in a single round. In a dungeon procedure, a zone is typically the size of a hallway or a room. In an exploration procedure, a zone is typically a single map tile. Zones are where the gameplay happens and the "boundaries" of a single round.
