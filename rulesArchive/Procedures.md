# Procedure

A `procedure` is a sequence of steps that define the process to play a section of the game. Each `procedure` has its own definition of a `round`, when it begins, and how long it lasts.

## Combat Procedure

The `combat procedure` starts when one or more `creatures` become aware of the presence of a hostile `creature` or dangerous `object` and ends when the danger clears. The map turns to the `dungeon map`, and each `round` lasts about 0.2 seconds.

## Exploration Procedure

The `exploration procedure` starts when traveling longer distances and ends when starting another `procedure`. The map turns to the `world map`, and each `round` lasts between 30 minutes and 3 hours (depending on the kind of terrain being traversed).

## Dungeon Procedure

The `dungeon procedure` starts when traveling carefully in a dangerous environment and ends when starting another `procedure`. The map turns to the `dungeon map`, and each `round` lasts about 10 minutes.

## Social Procedure

The `social procedure` starts when two `creatures` engage in conversation and ends when the conversation ends. The map (if one is necessary) turns to the `dungeon map`, and each `round` lasts the real time between breaks in the conversation.

## Downtime Procedure

The `downtime procedure` starts when one or more `creatures` decide to rest from dungeoneering and ends when cancelled or completed (not interrupted). The length and number of `rounds` depends on the type of `downtime`.

## Chase Procedure

The `chase procedure` starts when one group of `creatures` flees and another group pursues and ends when one side gives up. The map turns to the `dungeon map`, and each `round` lasts about 6 seconds.

# Rounds

A `round` is when all `creatures` involved in the `procedure` utilize their `action`. The duration of a `round` depends on the `procedure` it is in. At the start of a `round`, all `creatures` declare their `action`, and the `actions` resolve in order of `creature` `speed` (ties favor the player).
